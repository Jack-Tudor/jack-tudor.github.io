Who Am I and What Am I Working On?


I am a PhD student in the School of Social Sciences at Swansea University. My primary research interests centre on sources of error in pre-election polling, exploring in particular the multi-level decomposition of polling error and the importance of election-level factors in instances of misprediction. My work in these areas has been published in The Journal of Elections, Public Opinion and Parties, as well as online as part of UK Election Analysis, 2019: Media, Voters and the Campaign. Early Reflections from Leading Academics.

I have applied my training in statistics and data analysis to a wide range of research areas, including the creation of The Global Drugs Policy Index whilst working as a research assistant for the Global Drugs Policy Observatory. Research projects that I have been part of have been featured in The Guardian, The Telegraph, The New Zealand Herald, and The Times of India, as well as being reported on by Channel 4 News.

To date, my research has identified that election-level differences are a particularly important driver of pre-election polling error variance. My doctoral thesis further investigates this avenue of research by analysing the importance of election-level variables for the prediction of polling error, finding their presence useful in the reduction of root mean square error.

Moving forward, my research agenda concerns further investigation of the influence of the election level on polling error, as well as progressing towards a deeper, system-level understanding of elections as phenomena and its ramifications for electoral forecasting.
